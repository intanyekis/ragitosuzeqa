Scrub Daddy Origins

Aaron Krause’s journey as an inventor and entrepreneur took a pivotal turn when he stumbled upon the idea that would become Scrub Daddy. Prior to founding Scrub Daddy, Krause operated a buffing pad and car detailing company. However, the constant challenge of dealing with dirty hands led him to search for a more comfortable and effective cleaning method.

In 2007, after numerous experiments and iterations, Krause invented a polymer foam hand scrubber that would later serve as the foundation for Scrub Daddy. Initially, the concept faced resistance, and Krause put it aside. However, in 2011, he rediscovered the scrubbers and recognized their potential. This marked the beginning of the Scrub Daddy journey.

The invention of the polymer foam hand scrubber was a breakthrough for Krause. Its unique texture and versatility made it ideal for various cleaning tasks, from dishes and countertops to bathroom tiles. The innovative design, combined with its ability to change texture with water temperature, set Scrub Daddy apart in the market.
